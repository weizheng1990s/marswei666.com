在巴黎街头，我想起了深圳 On the Streets of Paris, I Thought of Shenzhen

两座城市，一个以历史定义自己，一个以速度证明自己。走在奥斯曼大道上，我突然理解了城市规划对人心理的塑造。 Two cities — one defined by history, the other proven by speed. Walking along Boulevard Haussmann, I suddenly understood how urban planning shapes the human mind.

第一次来巴黎是2007年，那时候我刚考上北京的大学，还没离开过中国。第二次是读书，住在蒙帕纳斯附近一间六楼的小屋子，没有电梯，冬天没有暖气。现在是第三次，我已经在深圳生活了八年，然后又走了两年。 My first time in Paris was 2007, when I had just been admitted to a university in Beijing and had never left China. The second time was for school — I lived on the sixth floor of a small room near Montparnasse, no elevator, no heating in winter. Now it's the third time. I've lived in Shenzhen for eight years, then wandered for two more.

站在奥斯曼大道上，我意识到这条路和深圳的深南大道有一种奇怪的相似——都是宽阔的、笔直的、刻意的。但感受完全不同。走在深南大道上，你会感到一种向前冲的压力，仿佛停下来就是落后；走在奥斯曼大道上，你感到的是一种被注视的感觉，一种布景的庄重。 Standing on Boulevard Haussmann, I noticed a strange resemblance to Shenzhen's Shennan Avenue — both wide, straight, deliberate. But the feeling is completely different. Walking down Shennan Avenue, you feel a pressure to push forward, as if stopping means falling behind. On Haussmann, you feel watched, staged — a certain grandeur of backdrop.

城市是一种论点 A City Is an Argument

奥斯曼男爵在1853年开始改造巴黎，拆掉中世纪的迷宫小巷，建造宽阔的林荫大道。目的之一，是让军队可以快速穿越市区镇压起义。这段历史很少被旅游手册提起。 Baron Haussmann began renovating Paris in 1853, tearing down medieval alleyways to build wide boulevards. One purpose was to allow troops to move quickly through the city to suppress uprisings. This history rarely appears in travel guides.

深圳则是另一种论点。它想证明一个城市可以在四十年内从渔村变成一线城市。它成功了，但这个速度的代价，是没有人真正属于这里。每个人都是过客，包括在那里生活了八年的我。 Shenzhen is a different kind of argument. It wants to prove a city can go from fishing village to first-tier metropolis in forty years. It succeeded — but the cost of that speed is that nobody truly belongs here. Everyone is a passerby, including me, who lived there for eight years.

这也许就是为什么，巴黎让人感到沉重，而深圳让人感到焦虑。一个城市背负太多过去，另一个城市没有足够的过去。 Maybe that's why Paris feels heavy, and Shenzhen feels anxious. One city carries too much past; the other doesn't have enough.

读《人类简史》：我们是故事动物 Reading Sapiens: We Are Storytelling Animals

赫拉利说，让智人统治世界的，不是工具，而是讲故事的能力。这让我重新审视自己每天消费的那些叙事。 Harari argues it wasn't tools but storytelling that gave Sapiens dominion over the world. This made me re-examine the narratives I consume every day.

我在东京的一家咖啡馆里读完了《人类简史》的最后一章。窗外是涩谷的人流，密集而有秩序，每个人都在扮演某个故事里的角色。 I finished the last chapter of Sapiens in a coffee shop in Tokyo. Outside the window, the crowds of Shibuya moved with dense precision — each person playing a role in some story.

赫拉利的核心论点是：智人之所以能在众多人类物种中胜出，是因为我们能够相信并传播"虚构的故事"——国家、货币、公司、宗教，这些都是集体想象的产物。七万年前，我们学会了讲故事，从此没有什么能阻止我们。 Harari's core argument: Sapiens prevailed among human species because we can believe and spread "fictional stories" — nations, money, companies, religions, all products of collective imagination. Seventy thousand years ago, we learned to tell stories, and nothing has stopped us since.

我们活在别人的故事里 We Live Inside Other People's Stories

读这本书让我开始审视：我每天消费的内容，哪些是事实，哪些是精心构建的叙事？新闻、社交媒体、公司战略，都是故事。问题不是故事本身，而是我有没有意识到自己在听故事。 Reading this book made me examine: of everything I consume daily, what is fact, and what is carefully constructed narrative? News, social media, corporate strategy — all stories. The question isn't the story itself, but whether I'm aware I'm being told one.

互联网大厂离职之后 After Leaving Big Tech

华为和腾讯教会了我什么，又让我付出了什么代价。当我终于停下来，才发现那 8 年在我身上留下的印记。 What Huawei and Tencent taught me, and what it cost. When I finally stopped, I saw the marks those 8 years had left on me.

离职的那天下午，我坐在深圳湾公园的长椅上，看着海。脑子里转的不是自由，而是：明天不用开早会了，这件事本身让我感到一种奇怪的空洞。 The afternoon I left, I sat on a bench at Shenzhen Bay Park, watching the sea. What ran through my mind wasn't freedom — it was: no morning standup tomorrow. That thought alone left a strange hollow feeling.

八年的大厂生活给了我很多：解决复杂问题的方法论，在混乱中保持结构的能力，以及一种对规模的直觉感知。但它也悄悄带走了一些东西：慢慢思考的习惯，做"没有用"的事情的勇气，以及对无聊的容忍。 Eight years in big tech gave me a lot: methodology for solving complex problems, the ability to maintain structure in chaos, and an intuitive sense of scale. But it also quietly took things away: the habit of slow thought, the courage to do "useless" things, and tolerance for boredom.

效率是一种成瘾 Efficiency Is an Addiction

大厂训练你把一切都转化为可量化的结果。这很有用，但长期下来，你会开始用同样的逻辑衡量生活的每一分钟。散步有什么产出？读闲书的 ROI 是多少？这种思维方式很难关掉。 Big tech trains you to convert everything into quantifiable outcomes. That's useful — but over time you start applying the same logic to every minute of life. What's the output of a walk? What's the ROI of reading fiction? That mode of thinking is hard to turn off.

离开之后，我花了将近半年才重新学会"什么都不做"。这不是懒惰，而是让大脑有机会产生真正属于自己的想法，而不是响应别人的需求。 After leaving, it took me nearly six months to relearn how to "do nothing." Not laziness — but giving the brain a chance to generate thoughts that were truly its own, rather than responses to someone else's needs.
